Flow pushed

# senario 3.
H2 in Vlan1 connected to switch 1 should be able to ping H7 in Vlan2 which is connected to switch 2 

Idea:  If a packet with the Ip address source Ip = 10.0.0.2 and destination Ip = 10.0.0.7 I am pushing a rule such that every packet that matches the above conditions at switch 1 is outputed to switch 2
Now at switch 2 I am changing the packet vlan Tag from 100 to 200 so that it is reachable to 10.0.0.7 which is vlan 2 
I am pushing two more rules vise-versa so that they can reach each other.

Flows pushed: 
sh ovs-ofctl add-flow s1 priority=100,ip,nw_src=10.0.0.2,nw_dst=10.0.0.7,actions=output:5
sh ovs-ofctl add-flow s2 priority=100,ip,nw_src=10.0.0.2,nw_dst=10.0.0.7,actions=mod_vlan_vid:200,output:3
sh ovs-ofctl add-flow s2 priority=100,ip,nw_src=10.0.0.7,nw_dst=10.0.0.2,actions=output:5
sh ovs-ofctl add-flow s1 priority=100,ip,nw_src=10.0.0.7,nw_dst=10.0.0.2,actions=mod_vlan_vid:100,output:2
